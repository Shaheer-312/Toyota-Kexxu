SSD Connections

On Ubuntu, your external SSD is usually mounted automatically under /media/. To find the exact path, plug in your SSD and run:
lsblk

Instead of saving to your laptop's internal drive and moving it later, you tell your script to write to the SSD path immediately.


    Camera: 500mA 

    Front Camera: 500mA 

    External SSD: 500mA - 900mA (standard for portable SSDs).

The Problem: If you plug both cameras and the SSD into the same side of your laptop or a cheap unpowered hub, you are asking for ~2.0 Amps of power. Most laptop USB ports are only rated for 0.9 Amps.

The Result: The SSD will "brown out" and disconnect in the middle of your recording, or one of the cameras will freeze.


The SOP Fix:

    Use a Powered USB Hub (one that plugs into a wall outlet) to connect everything.

    Alternatively, plug the SSD into one side of the laptop and the Cameras into the other side (different internal controllers).
